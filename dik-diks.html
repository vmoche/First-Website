Dik-diks

The dik-dik is a type of dwarf antelope that lives in arid regions of East Africa. They can live in such barren environments because they get water the vegetation they ear. They also take cover in undergrowth to protect themselves from predators,including eagles and cats.
There are four species of dik-diks, but just two of them live in Kenya.These include Guenther's dik-dik and Kirk's dik-dik.Kirk's is the largest dik-dik,though it's still small in comparison to other antelopes.It stands at just 45 centimetres(18 inches)tall and weighs only 7.2 kilograms(15.8 pounds).
Guenther's dik-dik is smaller but has a longer snout.The IUCN lists both Kirk's dik-dik Africa-and Guenther's dik-dik as least concern,though they are threatened by expandning agricultural settlements and excessive hunting in some areas.
