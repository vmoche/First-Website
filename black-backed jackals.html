Black-backed Jackals

The black-backed jackal is a member of the dog family.It has rusty red coat with a black patch on its back and a black-tipped tail.It measures around 95 centimetres(37 inches)from head to tail.
As opportunistic omnivores,jackals eat what they can.They work in family packs to hunt small antelopes,but they'll also feed on reptiles,insects,birds,fruits,berries and carcasses left by larger predators.
There are two distinct populations of black-backed jackals in Africa-one in East Africa,in countries including Namibia,Angola and South Africa.
These animals live in a wide variety of habitats,from deserts to grasslands to savannahs.The IUCN lists black-backed jackals as least concern,meaning they face few threats.
However,they are hunted by farmers who kills them to protect their livestock.
